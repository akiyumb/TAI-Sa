About Us

About Us

TAI-Sa (Tradition and Arts for Isolo and Science) is a non-profit community-based organisation (CBO), Reg. No. MDC/CBO/4196, based in Magu District, Mwanza, Tanzania. We support young science students, promote traditional games like Mancala, and enhance educational opportunities in rural areas. The CBO is led by Mr. Amos Kiyumbi, a PhD candidate at Stellenbosch University and an academic staff at the University of Dar es Salaam (UDSM), we aim to train the next generation in nanotechnology and photonics.

Founding Story

TAI-Sa was founded in 2020 by Amos Kiyumbi to bridge the gap in rural education and preserve cultural heritage through Mancala and science. His vision was to combine traditional knowledge with modern scientific education to empower rural youth.

Our Board

Our board includes local leaders and educators dedicated to advancing TAI-Sa's mission in Magu District. They provide guidance and support to ensure our projects align with community needs.

Our Team

A passionate group of volunteers and professionals, including teachers and science enthusiasts, drive our projects. Their expertise and dedication are key to our success.

Our Projects

Bed Project for Isendelo Secondary School

Providing 20 beds for 40 female students preparing for national exams, ensuring a safe sleeping environment to boost academic success.

Science and Mancala Training

Training rural students in science and traditional Mancala games to foster critical thinking and cultural preservation.

Our long-term plan

Build and run a tuition-free TAI-Sa Science boarding school for financially disadvantaged students and young science frontiers in rural/village areas.
